How can you prevent mobile voting from being hacked?
Today’s election technology is due for an upgrade.
  • Older computerized systems are likely to fail and are difficult to maintain.
  • Many current voting machines use outdated hardware and software that is no longer serviced.
  • Election officials know outdated voting machines should be replaced, but lack the funds.
That being said, security is a top priority for us. Our mobile voting system includes:
  • Multi-factor authentication that affirms the person voting is a real, registered voter.
  • Tamper proof read-only data storage ensuring data is not tampered with during the voting process or in transit.
  • Seamless integration with existing systems to prevent voters from voting more times than allowed.
  • Optional generation of backup paper ballots for verification and auditing purposes.
+
Who benefits from mobile voting?
Everyone! The Voting App is a cost-effective and scalable voting method for jurisdictions. In addition, voters everywhere will be able to conveniently cast their ballots, without sacrificing security or privacy. Specifically, the Voting App improves the voting experience for:
  • Voters with disabilities - In 2016, a federal analysis of access to 178 polling places found that 60% had one or more potential impediments for people with disabilities, including steep ramps, poor parking, and lack of signs indicating accessible paths. For many citizens with physical disabilities, voting in-person creates barriers to access and makes it difficult, if not impossible, to vote. Until now, these citizens have been left with few options to exercise their right to vote secretly.
  • Military and overseas voters - People who put their lives on the line to protect our freedom deserve to cast their votes privately, conveniently, and securely. Currently, the majority of military and overseas voters rely on mail, email, or fax to receive and return their ballots. This is extremely cumbersome and oftentimes means sacrificing privacy.
  • Non-native English speakers - Voters with limited English proficiency must rely on interpretation assistance from poll workers or friends and family. The Voting App offers customizable multi-language support, allowing voters of all backgrounds to vote independently and confidently.

Will mobile voting replace other voting methods?
The Voting App will expand voters’ options, not limit them. We believe mobile voting should be offered as an approved voting method alongside in-person and mail voting.

Is mobile voting secure? How do we protect against hacking and other security threats?
Like any great innovation, mobile voting faces a lot of scrutiny and justified concerns. Security is a top priority for us. We’ve reviewed the criticisms of internet voting in detail and made sure that our Voting App is a significant security upgrade from the digital systems currently available.

Through our tamper proof, read-only data storage with verifiable audit trail, we can ensure: 
  • The person voting is a real, registered voter
  • Data is not tampered with, during the voting process or in transit between components of the system
  • That once collected, votes cannot be manipulated
  • Voters are prevented from voting more times than allowed
+
You mentioned “audit trail”. Would the voting app produce a paper audit trail?
Yes, jurisdictions can insist on implementations that produce hardcopy artifacts. They also have the added bonus that an individual ballot stored in our digital ballot box is the electronic equivalent of a hardcopy paper ballot.
+
How would the voting app know if the person voting is a real, registered voter?
First, a voter downloads the app, and identifies which election they are attempting to vote in, similar to how one might look up a polling location.  

Then, the app authenticates the voter on the backend using a cryptographically unique signature, and that’s made up of two parts: 
  • First— Registration information provided by the administering jurisdiction; something only you should know (I.e., driver's license number, street address, or SSN) 
  • Second—A unique physical token (I.e., A randomly generated unique QR code that you receive by mail) 
Once you provide both pieces of information, you are authenticated as the voter, and the ballot you’re assigned gets delivered to your device so you can mark it.
+
Is your voting app blockchain? Why should blockchain be used for voting?
Vidaloop is tech agnostic. The properties of blockchain lend themselves well to voting, today, but we’re open to other technology if it’s proven to be superior in the future.

Our digital ballot box is based on an open source, enterprise grade blockchain that’s created and maintained by Intel called Hyperledger Sawtooth.

We’re building our digital ballot box on blockchain because it allows mutual distrusting parties to come to the same conclusion about the results. Some unique properties:
  • Immutable/read-only
  • Non corruptible data storage
  • Tamper proof
  • Transparent
  • Distributed operation (builds trust)
  • Fault Tolerant
+
Will this app be open source?
Yes several components of our mobile voting system will be open source. In addition, we have tools that allow third party security experts and election observers direct observational access to aspects of the election process previously more difficult to observe.
+
How do you protect voter privacy?
Cryptographically unique signatures are created in an air gapped system (not connected to the internet), and you can’t deduce any personal information from that. What’s stored on the blockchain is the signature associated with a correct ballot style. Observers into the blockchain can’t see who voted.
