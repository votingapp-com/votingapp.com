The future of voting technology

MODERN DAY VOTING

Transparent and trusted digital voting system.

It’s no secret that today’s voting technology is outdated. It’s time to modernize our election systems to meet the needs of American voters.
INCLUSIVE

Leading the way in accessibility

  • Exceeds the latest accessibility requirements with built-in features such as audio ballot, multilingual support, variable contrast rates and much more.
  • Uniform voting experience for all people, regardless of ability, situation, or context.
  • Voters can vote wherever and whenever they want without worrying about waiting on the mail.
TAMPER PROOF

Votes are safe and secure

  • Immutable data storage for all election content including ballots, voter lists, authentication requests and responses, returned votes, spoiled ballots.
  • Industry-vetted and accepted encryption technology for protecting sensitive data.
  • Follows mobile security best practices for securing devices and applications.
  • Custom made air gap device that handles security-sensitive processes.
Private

Protects the right to a secret ballot

  • Maintains voter privacy by never storing any identifiable voter information on any device.
  • Does not tie identifiable voter information to collected votes.
Auditable

Transparent and verifiable

  • All data is available publicly during pre-election, live election, and post election activities.
  • System is maintained during election cycle by groups of mutually distrustful parties forcing all parts of the system to perform honestly and accurately.

Frequently Asked Questions

You have questions we have answers.

Ask us a question
+
Is mobile voting secure? How do we protect against hacking and other security threats?
Like any great innovation, mobile voting faces a lot of scrutiny and justified concerns. Security is a top priority for us. We’ve reviewed the criticisms of internet voting in detail and made sure that our VotingApp is a significant security upgrade from the digital systems currently available.

Through our tamper proof, read-only data storage with verifiable audit trail, we can ensure: 
  • The person voting is a real, registered voter
  • Data is not tampered with, during the voting process or in transit between components of the system
  • That once collected, votes cannot be manipulated
  • Voters are prevented from voting more times than allowed
+
You mentioned “audit trail”. Would VotingApp produce a paper audit trail?
Yes, jurisdictions can insist on implementations that produce hardcopy artifacts. They also have the added bonus that an individual ballot stored in our digital ballot box is the electronic equivalent of a hardcopy paper ballot.
+
How would VotingApp know if the person voting is a real, registered voter?
First, a voter downloads the app, and identifies which election they are attempting to vote in, similar to how one might look up a polling location.  

Then, the app authenticates the voter on the backend using a cryptographically unique signature, and that’s made up of two parts: 
  • First— Registration information provided by the administering jurisdiction; something only you should know (I.e., driver's license number, street address, or SSN) 
  • Second—A unique physical token (I.e., A randomly generated unique QR code that you receive by mail) 
Once a voter provides both pieces of information, they are authenticated as the voter, and the ballot assigned gets delivered to their device.
+
Is VotingApp blockchain? Why should blockchain be used for voting?
Vidaloop is tech agnostic. The properties of blockchain lend themselves well to voting, today, but we’re open to other technology if it’s proven to be superior in the future.

Our digital ballot box is based on an open source, enterprise grade blockchain that’s created and maintained by Intel called Hyperledger Sawtooth.

We’re building our digital ballot box on blockchain because it allows mutual distrusting parties to come to the same conclusion about the results. Some unique properties:
  • Immutable/read-only
  • Non corruptible data storage
  • Tamper proof
  • Transparent
  • Distributed operation (builds trust)
  • Fault Tolerant
+
Will this app be open source?
Several components of our mobile voting system will be open source. In addition, we have tools that allow third party security experts and election observers direct observational access to aspects of the election process previously more difficult to observe.
+
How do you protect voter privacy?
Cryptographically unique signatures are created in an air gapped system (not connected to the internet), protecting personal information.  On the blockchain, observers can only see a signature associated with the correct ballot style.
