Voter Receipt

This page provides a high level description of the VotingApp’s Voter Receipt provided within the mobile application to voters.

What is the Voter Receipt?

Simply, the Voter Receipt is a code provided to each voter during the voting process on their mobile app that is unique to their ballot. This receipt is stored both on the voter’s device and within the encrypted ballot storage location called the Ledger.

How is the Voter Receipt created?

The Voter Receipt is a combination of your selections made during voting along with a unique bit of data you were asked to provide upon first receiving your ballot electronically. This unique bit of information was called the Voter Input Code and was either randomly generated by the system for you or that was overwritten by you at the beginning of the voting process with information you provided. Ideally, you did not include any identifying information if you chose to input the data yourself. Perhaps you used an old pet’s name, or a high school mascot, or any random set of words, numbers and characters you decided were appropriate.

The system then uses that random info along with each selection you make to run through an algorithm to make the Voter Receipt code. Because you included some random info it would be impossible for anyone with access to your Voter Receipt code to deconstruct it and discover your vote selection. However, because it is in part created with your selections it changes as you vote more contests. Don’t believe me? Try selecting a candidate and look in the upper left of the menu bar where the real-time Voter Receipt code is being displayed. Unselect the same candidate and watch the code change back to the previous code. In this way you can see how your activity in the application alters your Voter Receipt code.

What do I do with the Voter Receipt?

It will be stored on your device after you submit your vote. As part of that receipt you will have a QR code and a link that takes you to the Vote Tracker page. You can use this link to see the status of your stored Vote at anytime after you submit it.

What do the Statuses mean?

VotingApp provides you with information about your voted ballot throughout the election process. These statuses are as listed below:
  1. Submitted - This means you have successfully submitted an encrypted marked ballot to the Ledger. Along with your vote a copy of your receipt exactly as exists on your device is also stored on the Ledger. Your status will stay Submitted for the duration of open voting. Once the election has closed check back to see the status of your vote.
  2. Accepted - This means the election office has confirmed the validity of your stored vote. To learn more about this process see below in the section titled ‘How do I know my vote was counted correctly?'
  3. Rejected Signature - This means your election office has failed to match your affidavit signature with the one on file. Please call your election office to resolve the issue
  4. Rejected - This means your vote was rejected for other reasons such as the election office also received a paper ballot from you or you voted in person at a polling location. Finally, it could mean your vote was stored incorrectly and the problem was identified. See the section ‘How do I know my vote was counted correctly?’ for more information.

How do I know my vote was counted correctly?

Remember that receipt on your phone and how it was created? Well after the election closes your election office will take your encrypted vote (along with all the other collected votes as well), put them into an air-gapped device that holds the only decryption key for all votes. After your vote is decrypted the device will re-create your receipt in the exact same method as your device did originally, by combining your unique Voter Input Code along with your selections from your ballot. If that device can exactly recreate your receipt with those bits of information using the same algorithm your device used, you can be assured the selections you made on your device that you verified at voting time, are the same selections stored on the Ledger, and now decrypted for counting. If the receipt stored on the Ledger matches the re-created receipt ‘ACCEPTED’ is appended as a status to your vote. If those receipts do not match ‘REJECTED’ is appended. This should never happen in a properly running system that has not been tampered with. You may have your ballot Rejected for other reasons (see above) but if the system is operating properly it will not be rejected for mismatched receipts. The final check is up to you as a voter. You can check the receipt code store on your device matches the receipt code stored on the Ledger and viewable through the Vote Tracker by you.

This seems complicated, how do I really know it worked?

Yes, we know this process involves a lot of complicated calculation and perhaps concepts or specifics you are unfamiliar with. That’s okay, because we invite you to have anyone you trust that may have more experience with digital encryption, data encoding, and digital data transport and storage to take a look at the details of the system. The system is built around transparency and we believe voters only trust a system that they can vet themselves or have vetted by someone they trust. If someone you trust verifies the details of the inner workings, all you need do as a voter is keep track of that Voter Receipt and associated status. With those two pieces you can easily follow your ballot from the moment you cast it to the moment it’s counted officially. The best part, your vote remains anonymous. You don’t have to share your vote with anyone even if you choose to share the receipt, no one can tell how you voted.
